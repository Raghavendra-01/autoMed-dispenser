🔩 Hardware Analysis

Every component, examined

MICROCONTROLLER · SYSTEM BRAIN
ESP32-WROOM-32
Xtensa dual-core LX6 at 240MHz. The ESP32 is the orchestrator: reads DS3231 over I2C (GPIO21=SDA, GPIO22=SCL), generates 50Hz PWM to SG90 servo via GPIO27, drives SSD1306 OLED over I2C, and triggers the buzzer at GPIO5. All dispense events are written to 4MB flash memory as timestamped logs — fully retrievable via USB serial.
240MHz Dual-Core LX6I2C GPIO 21/22 PWM GPIO 27Buzzer GPIO 5 520KB SRAM4MB Flash 3.3V LogicUSB-C
REAL-TIME CLOCK · TIMING BACKBONE
DS3231 RTC Module
Temperature-compensated crystal oscillator (TCXO) with ±2ppm accuracy — less than 1 minute drift per year. Communicates with the ESP32 over I2C at up to 400kHz. The SQW/INT pin drives a GPIO interrupt on the ESP32, waking it from deep sleep the instant a dose is due. The onboard AT24C32 EEPROM stores alarm schedules. CR2032 backup cell maintains timekeeping through power outages.

ACTUATOR · DISPENSING MECHANISM
TowerPro SG90 Micro Servo
9-gram micro servo with 1.8 kg·cm stall torque at 4.8V. Controlled by the ESP32 via 50Hz PWM: 1ms pulse = 0°, 2ms pulse = 180°. Each dispense event rotates the drum precisely 60° (360° ÷ 6 compartments). The nylon gear train ensures quiet, reliable actuation. Powered directly from the 5V rail — separate from the 3.3V logic supply — preventing voltage sag on the ESP32.
50Hz PWM (1–2ms)60° per dispense step 1.8 kg·cm @ 4.8V180° total range 4.8–6V supply9g weight Nylon gear train3-wire (PWM/5V/GND)
POWER MANAGEMENT · REGULATED DISTRIBUTION
Custom Power Distribution Board
5V DC wall adapter feeds through a 2A polyfuse and 1N4007 reverse-polarity diode into the input rail. The AMS1117-3.3 LDO regulator (max 800mA) supplies all 3.3V logic components — ESP32, DS3231, and OLED. The SG90 servo draws directly from the 5V rail via a dedicated header, preventing motor current spikes from affecting the logic supply. 100µF bulk decoupling and 100nF bypass capacitors ensure stable rail voltages.
